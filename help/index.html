Why pay-what-you-want?

Most "free strategy calls" are pitches wearing a costume. You book, you show up, and 40 minutes later someone is selling you a retainer. I hated being on the other end of those, so I'm not running one.

Here's the honest version. I'm good at this stuff and I like helping people get unstuck. The fastest way to prove that is to actually help you first and let you decide what it was worth after. If I'm useful, you pay what feels fair. If I'm not, you walk away and pay nothing. No hard feelings.

That's the whole model. The only catch is there is no catch.

What's the actual catch? +

There isn't one. We talk, I help, and after the call you decide what it was worth and pay that. If it didn't help, you pay nothing and that's fine.

How much do people usually pay? +

Whatever the help was worth to them. Some pay a little, some pay a lot, some pay nothing because it wasn't a fit. You're in charge of that number, after you know if it helped.

What if I don't have anything prepared? +

Then just tell me roughly what you're stuck on in the form. A messy half-formed problem is still something I can work with. Bring it as-is.

What if my thing isn't on your list? +

Pick "Something else" and tell me what it is. If it's not my area, I'll tell you straight and point you somewhere better. No charge for honesty.

How long is the call? +

Long enough to make real progress on your problem. We're not watching a clock. If we get you unstuck in 15 minutes, great.

Is this a sales pitch for something bigger? +

No. There's nothing to upsell on the call. If you want more help after, you can ask. I won't bring it up.
